# Data Analyst

I think of myself as a Data Artisan—someone who doesn’t just analyze data, but carefully shapes it into something meaningful.

I enjoy working hands-on with messy, complex datasets and turning them into clear, practical insights that people can actually use. Instead of relying on generic, one-size-fits-all solutions, I take a more tailored approach—understanding the context, asking the right questions, and crafting analysis that fits the specific business problem.

My work sits at the intersection of data and decision-making. I’m comfortable getting deep into the technical side—cleaning, transforming, and exploring data—but what really drives me is making that work useful. I focus on translating complexity into clarity so stakeholders can move forward with confidence.

I don’t come at data as an abstract exercise—I see it as a tool for solving real problems, improving processes, and uncovering opportunities that might otherwise go unnoticed.

In a world where analytics is often automated and scaled, I bring a more thoughtful, detail-oriented approach—because sometimes the most valuable insights are the ones that are carefully crafted.
